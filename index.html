1.Ragi Laddu — Jaggery & Ghee

Simple, wholesome laddus made with ragi flour, jaggery and ghee — high in calcium, fibre and natural energy.

Yield: 10–12 laddus • Prep: 10 min • Cook: 10 min

Ingredients
  • 1 cup ragi flour (finger millet)
  • ¾ cup powdered jaggery
  • 3–4 tbsp ghee (warm)
  • Optional: ¼ tsp cardamom powder, 1–2 tbsp chopped nuts (almonds/pista/cashews)
Step-by-step Method
  1. Dry roast ragi flour:

    Heat a pan on low flame. Add 1 cup ragi flour and roast for 6–8 minutes until aromatic. Stir continuously to avoid burning.
  2. Add ghee:

    Add 3–4 tbsp warm ghee to the roasted flour. Mix until the texture turns slightly moist and crumbly.
  3. Add jaggery:

    Turn off the heat. Add ¾ cup powdered jaggery and mix well so the jaggery blends into the warm flour. If needed, add 1 tsp warm water to help melt the jaggery.
  4. Flavour & nuts (optional):

    Add cardamom powder and chopped nuts. Mix thoroughly.
  5. Shape laddus:

    While warm, take small portions and press between palms to shape round laddus. Add a little ghee if mixture is dry.
  6. Cool & store:

    Let laddus cool completely. Store in an airtight container for 7–10 days at room temperature.

Storage

Keep in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 7–10 days. For longer storage, refrigerate (up to 3 weeks).

2.Simple Nuts Smoothie

Almond • Cashew • Raisins • Pista • Boiled Milk — Fig & Dates optional

Ingredients — makes 2 servings
  • Almonds — 40 g (about 10–12 whole almonds)
  • Cashews — 30 g (about 8–10 cashews)
  • Pistachios (pista) — 30 g (about 10–12 pistachios, shelled)
  • Raisins — 30 g (about 2 tablespoons)
  • Boiled whole milk — 500 ml (warm or cooled, as you prefer)
  • Honey or jaggery (optional) — 1–2 tsp to taste
  • Ice cubes — a few (optional, for chill)
  • Optional: Dates or dried figs — 1–2 large dates (≈40 g) or 1 medium dried fig — for extra sweetness & texture
  • Pinch of cardamom powder or a drop of vanilla (optional, for flavor)
Tip: If you prefer a thinner smoothie, increase milk by 50–100 ml. For a vegan version, use almond milk instead of boiled cow's milk and omit or replace honey.
Step-by-step Method
  1. Soak the nuts & raisins (quick soak):

    Place the almonds, cashews, pistachios and raisins in a small bowl. Cover with warm water and soak 20–30 minutes to soften. (If you're short on time you can skip soaking, but soaked nuts blend creamier.)
  2. Drain & peel (optional): Drain the soaking water. If you like a very smooth texture, peel the almonds by pinching — they should slip out easily after soaking.
  3. Add to blender:

    Put the soaked nuts, raisins, (and dates/figs if using) into the blender jar.
  4. Pour boiled milk:

    Add 500 ml boiled (and slightly cooled) milk to the blender.
  5. Flavor & sweeten:

    Add a pinch of cardamom powder or a drop of vanilla. Add honey or jaggery only if you want extra sweetness — dates already sweeten the drink.
  6. Blend:

    Blend on high for 45–60 seconds until very smooth and slightly frothy. If it's too thick, add 50–100 ml more milk and blend again.
  7. Adjust & serve:

    Taste and adjust sweetness. Add a few ice cubes and give a quick pulse if you want it chilled. Pour into two glasses and garnish with crushed pistachio or a small almond slice.
Ready in ~10–40 minutes depending on soaking time. Serves 2.

Health Benefits
  • Heart-healthy fats: Almonds, cashews and pistachios provide monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats that support heart health.
  • Protein boost: Nuts plus milk offer a good mix of plant & animal protein — helpful for satiety and muscle repair.
  • Minerals & vitamins: Nuts are rich in magnesium, vitamin E (almonds), phosphorus and potassium; milk adds calcium and vitamin D (if fortified).
  • Natural energy: Raisins and dates give quick natural sugars plus fibre for sustained energy — great as a breakfast or post-workout drink.
  • Digestive support: The fibre from nuts, raisins and dates supports healthy digestion.
  • Antioxidants: Nuts and dried fruits contain polyphenols and antioxidants that help reduce oxidative stress.
Note: This smoothie is calorie-dense — excellent as a nourishing breakfast or snack, but if you're watching calories eat it in moderation or reduce nut/milk quantities.
Storage
  • Short-term: Store in an airtight container or jar in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. Shake or stir well before serving — natural separation may occur.
  • Freezing: Not recommended after blending (texture changes). You may freeze soaked nuts (before blending) in a freezer-safe bag for up to 1 month and blend fresh when needed.
  • To keep it fresh: Add ice only when serving. If storing, keep chilled and consume next day for best taste and food-safety.
